Conclusion

FTP has been in use for many decades as an effective file transfer protocol. While new technologies have emerged over the years, FTP remains a commonly used technology. In this written report, we explored the history and applications of FTP.

We also examined the message type, message syntax, message sequence and connection type in the context of FTP. We looked at how the use of traditional FTP presents significant security risks due to the susceptibility of the authentication information and messages being intercepted.

More recent iterations of the protocol add some security enhancements, which have served to extend the shelf life of the technology. Due to its relative ease of use, FTP will likely remain in use in some form another into the future.
